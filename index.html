The flip side 🌱

This isn't about never having nice things. It's about understanding that every rupee is a choice — and the money has to come from somewhere first.

Here's the hopeful part: if future-you simply saved ₹5,000 a month from age 27 and invested it at ~12% a year, by age 47 it could grow to roughly ₹50 lakh. Small, boring choices quietly become big ones.

A great life isn't the one with the biggest cart. It's the one where your spending matches a real plan you actually built.

Think it through

  1. Which three things in your cart are truly worth working hard for — and which were just "nice to look at"?
  2. What kind of work or skill could realistically pay the salary your dream year needs?
  3. If you earned exactly what you expect to, what would you happily give up — and what would you keep?
  4. What's one money habit you could start now, at your age, that future-you would thank you for?
